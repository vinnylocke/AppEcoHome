The Rhozly reply template — what "consistency" is judged against

Baked into the assistant's system prompt (agent-chat/rules.ts); canonical copy in reply-template.md.

Every Garden AI answer follows the same five-part shape, so a gardener always knows

where to look. Parts 2–5 appear only when they apply — a simple factual question

gets part 1 (and maybe 5) alone, in one or two sentences.

1. Bottom line — one plain sentence that directly answers the question or states

what has been prepared. Always first. Never a heading, never a preamble.

2. Detail bullets — only when the answer genuinely has parts (max ~6):

  • When: late summer, right after flowering
  • How: cut back a third, never into old wood
  • Watch for: frost on new growth

Short bold labels (When / How / How much / Why / Watch for) so the eye can jump

straight to the fact it needs.

3. Garden line — only when the assistant read the user's data this turn:

🔎 Checked: your Shed (2 tomatoes) · this week's tasks · weather

4. Action line — only when an action was staged for confirmation:

🔧 Ready to confirm: water Raised Bed A & B every 3 days

When a follow-up step depends on this one (e.g. linking an ailment that's being

added), the line also says what will be staged right after the user confirms.

5. Next step — at most one offer or question, on its own line:

→ Want me to set up a fortnightly feeding reminder too?

Why this shape

  • Bottom-line-first means nobody reads a paragraph to find a yes/no.
  • Labelled bullets make facts scannable and consistent across every answer.
  • 🔎 / 🔧 / → are fixed markers: what was *read*, what's *ready to apply*, what

*could happen next* — the same place, every reply.

  • One offer max keeps replies calm — no stacked questions.

Q How often should I water a peace lily?
Peace lilies prefer consistently moist soil, so it's best to water them when the top inch of soil feels dry to the touch.

- **The Droop Test:** This plant is famously dramatic and will droop its leaves noticeably when it's thirsty. It's often better to wait for a slight droop than to overwater.
- **How to Water:** Water thoroughly until it runs out of the drainage holes at the bottom of the pot. Tip out any excess water from the saucer so the plant isn't left sitting in it.
- **Frequency:** This might be roughly once a week in the growing season (spring and summer) and less often in winter, but always check the soil first rather than sticking to a rigid schedule.
- **Water Quality:** Peace lilies can be sensitive to chemicals in tap water. If you notice brown leaf tips, try using filtered, distilled, or rainwater.

Q My tomato leaves are turning yellow — what's wrong?
Yellowing tomato leaves usually point to a watering imbalance or a nutrient deficiency, though it can also just be the natural aging of the lowest leaves.

- **Watering:** Check the soil a couple of inches down — if it's soggy, hold off on watering so the roots can breathe; if it's bone dry, give it a deep soak.

Q Why are my courgette flowers falling off without fruiting?
This is usually a pollination problem, which is very common with courgettes.

- **Male & Female Flowers:** Courgette plants have separate male and female flowers. The female flowers are the ones that produce fruit, and you can identify them by the tiny immature courgette (a swelling) at their base. Male flowers grow on a long, thin stem and appear first.
- **Pollination:** For the tiny fruit to develop, pollen must be transferred from a male flower to a female flower, usually by bees. If a female flower isn't pollinated, the plant aborts the fruit and the flower falls off.
- **Common Causes:** A lack of pollinators is the most common reason. This can be due to cold, wet, or windy weather that keeps bees away. Sometimes, the plant produces lots of male flowers before the female ones start appearing.
- **How to Fix It:** You can become the bee! In the morning when the flowers are open, pick a male flower, strip off its petals, and gently dab the pollen from the central anther onto the stigma in the centre of a female flower.
