# Notes

**Notes** is your garden notebook. Jot down anything — observations, ideas, plant care reminders, sketches of new beds — and link them to the plants, areas, and plans they belong to. Find them later from any of those screens.

Access via the **Garden** tab → **Notes**, or directly at `/notes`.

> 📸 Screenshot: The Notes page showing the search bar at top, a row of pinned notes, and a grid of "All notes" below

---

## What Notes Do

Notes are free-form rich text. They are deliberately not tasks, not schedules, not plans — they're the loose end of your garden brain. Use them for:

- **Observations** — "First tomato flowered today" / "Aphids on the kale, treated"
- **Ideas you don't want to forget** — "Try wildflower strip along south fence next year"
- **Care reminders that aren't reminders** — "Bob said this rose prefers Sunday feeding"
- **Shopping thoughts** — "Need more compost bin liners before October"
- **Sketches and clippings** — paste images, add a header, save

Anything you would have written on the back of an envelope.

---

## Writing a Note

Tap **+ New Note** at the top of the page.

> 📸 Screenshot: The Note Editor with a title field and the rich-text body area showing the formatting toolbar

The editor opens. Type a title (optional) and start writing. The toolbar lets you:

| Format | Use it for |
|--------|-----------|
| **Heading** | Big section labels |
| **Bullet list** | A list of things |
| **Numbered list** | A sequence |
| **Checkbox list** | A mini-to-do you can tick |
| **Table** | A small grid of data |
| **Image** | Paste, drag, or upload a picture |
| **Bold / italic / link** | The usual rich-text trio |

Notes auto-save as you type — no Save button. Close the editor with the **×** in the corner.

---

## Linking a Note to a Plant, Area, or Plan

The most useful thing about Notes is the **link panel** at the bottom of the editor.

> 📸 Screenshot: The link picker at the bottom of the note editor showing the search field and a list of plants, areas, and plans

Tap **+ Link** to open the link picker:

1. Search for a plant, area, or plan by name.
2. Tap the result to attach it.
3. Repeat for as many entities as you like — notes can link to many things at once.

Now, when you open that plant's page, area details, or plan, the note shows up under a **Notes** section. So the next time you're staring at your tomatoes wondering why they did so well last August, the answer ("fed weekly with comfrey tea") is right there.

To unlink, tap the **×** on any attached chip in the editor.

---

## Pinning a Note

Tap the **📌 Pin** icon on any note card (or in the editor) to keep it at the top of the Notes page. Pinned notes appear in a dedicated row above the regular feed.

> 📸 Screenshot: The Notes page with a row of pinned notes above the regular feed

Useful for: standing reminders ("Frost dates 2026"), recurring observations ("Lawn problem zones"), or your gardening rules of thumb.

---

## Searching and Filtering

The search bar at the top of the page searches both **title and body** in real time. Type any keyword to filter the feed.

Notes don't have tags — they have links. Use a plant's page to see every note touching that plant; use an area's page to see every note about that area.

---

## Archiving and Deleting

Inside the editor, the **⋯** menu offers:

| Action | What it does |
|--------|--------------|
| **Archive** | Removes the note from the feed; it's not deleted, just hidden. Find archived notes from the **Archived** tab. |
| **Delete** | Permanent. Confirms before removing. |

Restore archived notes from the **Archived** tab → tap **Restore** on any card.

---

## Common Questions

**Are notes shared with other home members?** Yes — every member of a home can read and write the same notes feed.

**Can I export a note?** Not yet directly. You can copy the body and paste it elsewhere.

**Why no tags?** Linking is sharper. A note about "watering" tagged to your tomato plant *and* the south bed area surfaces from both screens automatically — tags would have meant maintaining a category tree.
