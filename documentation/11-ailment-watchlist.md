# Ailment Watchlist

The **Ailment Watchlist** helps you track and manage pests, diseases, and invasive plants in your garden. Access it from the **Garden** tab → **Watchlist** sub-tab.

> 📸 Screenshot: The Ailment Watchlist page showing cards for three ailments — a pest (aphids), a disease (powdery mildew), and an invasive plant (bindweed)

---

## What is an Ailment?

An ailment is any recurring threat to your garden plants:

| Type | Examples |
|------|---------|
| **Pest** | Aphids, slugs, vine weevils, caterpillars, spider mites |
| **Disease** | Powdery mildew, blight, rust, root rot, botrytis |
| **Invasive Plant** | Bindweed, bramble, ground elder, Japanese knotweed |

---

## Ailment Cards

Each tracked ailment appears as a card.

> 📸 Screenshot: A single ailment card showing the name, type badge, severity badge, affected plants, and expand button

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Ailment name** | Common name of the pest/disease/plant |
| **Type badge** | Pest / Disease / Invasive Plant |
| **Severity badge** | Mild (yellow), Moderate (orange), or Severe (red) |
| **Affected plants** | List of plant instances linked to this ailment |
| **Status** | Active or Archived |

## Adding an Ailment

There are three ways to log a new ailment:

### Option 1: Manually

Tap **+ Log Ailment** (or use [Global Quick Add](./15-navigation-quick-add.md#global-quick-add) → **Log Ailment**).

> 📸 Screenshot: The Add Ailment form with fields for name, type, severity, and affected plants

Fill in:
- **Name** — the ailment name
- **Type** — Pest, Disease, or Invasive Plant
- **Severity** — Mild, Moderate, Severe
- **Affected plants** — select which plants in your Shed are affected
- **Symptoms** — describe what you're seeing (optional)

### Option 2: From the Plant Database (Perenual)

Tap **Search Database** in the Add Ailment form. Search for a known pest or disease by name — Rhozly looks it up in the Perenual database and pre-fills the ailment details including prevention and remedy steps.

### Option 3: Via Plant Doctor

When the [Plant Doctor AI](./08-plant-doctor.md) diagnoses a disease or pest from a photo, you can add the result directly to the Watchlist. The diagnosis auto-fills all ailment details.

## Prevention and Remedy Steps

Each ailment has its own structured **prevention** and **remedy** workflow.

> 📸 Screenshot: An expanded ailment showing the prevention steps and remedy steps as numbered sequences

**Prevention steps** — things you do to stop the ailment taking hold or spreading.

**Remedy steps** — active treatment actions once the ailment is present.

Each step includes:
- **Step title** (e.g. "Apply neem oil spray")
- **Description** with instructions
- **Task type** (so it can be added to your schedule)
- **Frequency** (how often this step should be repeated)
- **Product** (specific product name if applicable)
- **Duration** (how many days/weeks to apply treatment)

### Turning Steps into Tasks

Tap **Create Task** on any step to add it to your schedule. This creates a task (or blueprint if it's a recurring step) directly from the treatment plan.

## Building a Custom Ailment

Tap **Edit** on any ailment card to open the **Ailment Editor**, where you can build out the step sequences in detail.

> 📸 Screenshot: The Ailment Editor showing a multi-step prevention workflow being built with drag-to-reorder handles

- **Add step** — tap + to add a new prevention or remedy step
- **Edit step** — tap any step to modify its fields
- **Reorder** — drag steps up and down to change the sequence
- **Delete step** — swipe or tap the bin icon

### Symptom Tracker

Within the editor, you can also log specific symptoms:
- **Symptom name** (e.g. "White powder on leaves")
- **Severity** — Mild, Moderate, Severe
- **Location on plant** (e.g. Upper leaf surface, Stem base, Roots)

## Filtering the Watchlist

> 📸 Screenshot: The Watchlist filter bar showing type filter pills and a search field

**Filter by type:** All, Pest, Disease, Invasive Plant

**Search:** Type any part of the ailment name.

---

## Archiving an Ailment

Once you've dealt with a problem, tap **Archive** on the ailment card. This:
- Moves the ailment to the **Archived** section
- Removes it from active monitoring
- Keeps a record for future reference

Tap **Restore** on any archived ailment to bring it back to active status.

---

## Ailment Severity Guide

| Severity | When to use |
|----------|-------------|
| **Mild** | Early signs; limited spread; plants otherwise healthy |
| **Moderate** | Noticeable impact; spread to multiple plants or areas |
| **Severe** | Significant damage; risk of plant loss; immediate action needed |

Update the severity as the situation develops — escalate it if the problem is spreading, or reduce it as treatments take effect.
