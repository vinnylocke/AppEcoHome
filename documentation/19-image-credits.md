# Image Credits

Every plant photo, identification result, and care illustration in Rhozly comes from somewhere — Pl@ntNet contributors, Perenual's database, Verdantly, Unsplash photographers, or the Rhozly team. Rhozly **credits every one of them** so you can see at a glance where each image came from.

This guide explains the small **credit badge** that appears on plant tiles, what each provider's badge means, and where to find the full credits register.

---

## The Credit Badge

> 📸 Screenshot: A plant tile with a small "Pl@ntNet — Contributor Name (CC-BY-SA)" badge in the bottom-left corner

A credit badge appears on:

- **Plant tiles** in The Shed
- **Search rows** when you add a plant
- **Plant hero images** at the top of a plant detail page
- **Lightbox views** when you expand any image
- **Pl@ntNet result tiles** in [Plant Lens](./08-plant-doctor.md)
- **Visualiser sprites** that came from a public source

Tap the badge to open a popover with:

| Field | What it shows |
|-------|--------------|
| **Source** | Pl@ntNet / Perenual / Verdantly / Unsplash / Rhozly AI |
| **Contributor** | The original photographer or contributor's name |
| **Licence** | The licence under which the image is shared (e.g. CC-BY-SA, CC-BY, Unsplash licence) |
| **Link** | A "View original" link to the source site |

---

## What Each Provider Means

| Provider | What you get | Licence |
|----------|--------------|---------|
| **Pl@ntNet** | Community-uploaded ID photos for the Plant Lens dual-tile result. Real photos from real gardeners. | CC-BY-SA — share-and-credit |
| **Perenual** | The plant-database default for care info + thumbnails | Perenual API terms |
| **Verdantly** | Alternative database for species details | Verdantly's terms |
| **Unsplash** | High-quality stock images for plant heroes and sprite sources | Unsplash licence |
| **Rhozly AI** | AI-generated illustrations (Garden Overhaul concepts, sprite art) | Generative; no third-party attribution |

When you see a small **CC-BY-SA** chip on a badge, it means the image is shared under the Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike licence — you're free to reuse it with credit, but any derivative work needs the same licence.

---

## Why It Matters

Rhozly's plant images come from people — many of them volunteer naturalists who contributed photos to Pl@ntNet, or hobby photographers on Unsplash. Naming them isn't just legally required for some licences; it's the right thing to do.

The badge also doubles as a quality signal:

- **Pl@ntNet** images are real plant photos from a curated community
- **Unsplash** images are high-quality but not always botanically labelled
- **Rhozly AI** illustrations are stylised; great for sprites, less so for ID

If two sources offer the same plant, you can compare the badges before picking one.

## The /credits Page

For the full register of who supplied what across your plants, visit `/credits` from any plant detail page → tap **All image credits**.

![The Image Credits page — where every image in Rhozly comes from](/doc-images/19-image-credits-01-register.webp)

The page lists every credited image in your home with a thumbnail, contributor name, licence, and a link to the original. Filter by source or contributor to find a specific photographer's work.

---

## Tips

- **Hold the badge tap** — it opens the popover rather than navigating away, so you can read the credit without losing your place.
- **The same plant can have multiple credit badges** — one for the species photo, one for any user-uploaded photo, one for each Pl@ntNet match.
- **Your own uploaded photos** don't get badges. They're yours; no attribution needed.

---

## Common Questions

**Why are some plants in my Shed missing badges?** Plants you added manually (with no image) or AI-generated entries from Wave 19 or earlier may not have stored credits — Wave 22.0004 backfilled the worst of these but not every legacy row.

**Can I remove a badge?** No — credits are required by the upstream licences. If a particular badge looks wrong, contact support and we'll investigate the source.

**Does Rhozly use my photos elsewhere?** No. Photos you upload stay in your private storage and aren't shared with other users or used for AI training.
